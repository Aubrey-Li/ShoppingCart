Christmas

Shopping Cart




Application


Description

The application is designed for the Christmas shopping season, where customers could browse and purchase Christmas related items to their heart's content. The goal is to have a convenient and seamless shopping experience for users, as they can sort through different products, filter by size and category, and add items to the cart all on a single page.

Value

This web application is expected to function like a simplified version of Amazon in Christmas season or a e-commerce shop that provides retailers a platform to sell their products.






Design Principles



Summary

The design of the application follows the web design of a typical e-commerce store like Amazon, where the items are on the left hand side of the screen and the cart is on the right hand side in a sidebar. The filters and sort are on the top of the main page where the items are aligned. The number of items on the page is displayed on the left-hand side to provide a brief overview. There's also a default button where users can default the search in the default order instead of adjusting filters and sorts to go back to the original state.

The items are displayed as cards since it matches user's expectations and each card has the same format (image, title of product, price, sizes, category) to increase learnability and memorability. After adding the items to the cart, it is also displayed as a card but with less content so users can browse through more easily. The cards displaying the items also has the function to increase or decrease numbers of purchase, or remove the item if desired. This design is aimed to increase the efficiency of navigating the page by having essential and relevant functionalities close to one another.

For the color palette and font-size, I choose colors that are in the Christmas color palette and uses high contrast to highlight the main functionalities such as "Add to Cart", "Remove", or "Check Out." I choose the Nunito Font Family for the entire website to be visually consistent.
